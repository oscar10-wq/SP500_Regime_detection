Analaysis Features:

SPX_Close:

  S&P 500 index level representing aggregate US large-cap equity prices and forward expectations of growth and earnings.
  High levels → strong expected economic growth and earnings outlook.
  Falling levels → tightening financial conditions and recession risk.

SPX_Volume:

  Total trading activity in S&P 500 constituents measuring market participation and conviction.
  High volume → uncertainty, information arrival, or regime transition.
  Low volume → stable macro environment or complacency.
  Volume spikes often occur around policy or economic shocks.

SPX_ROC (Rate of Change):

  Price momentum measure capturing speed and direction of market trend.
  Positive momentum → improving growth expectations and expansion regime.
  Negative momentum → deteriorating outlook and economic slowdown risk.
  Reflects persistence of macroeconomic cycles.

SPX_RSI (Relative Strength Index):

  Bounded oscillator (0-100) measuring buying vs selling pressure and market sentiment extremes.
  High RSI → excessive optimism or potential asset overvaluation.
  Low RSI → panic, stress, or deleveraging conditions.
  Extremes often precede macro turning points.

SPX_MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence):

  Trend-following indicator measuring difference between short and long moving averages.
    - short MA - long MA: positive implies bullish, negative implies bearish
  Rising MACD → accelerating growth expectations and risk-on environment.
  Falling MACD → weakening economic outlook.
  Useful for identifying business cycle shifts.

VIX_Close:

  Implied volatility of S&P 500 options representing expected market uncertainty and risk.
  High VIX → financial stress, uncertainty, recession risk.
  Low VIX → stable growth expectations and loose financial conditions.
  Higher risk raises cost of capital and reduces investment.

Fed_Funds_Future:

  Market expectation of future Federal Reserve policy interest rates.
  Rising expectations → tighter monetary policy and slower growth.
  Falling expectations → economic stimulus or recession response.
  Directly affects borrowing costs, credit conditions, and demand.

10Y_Treasury_Future:

  Futures price on 10-year US government bonds reflecting long-term growth and inflation expectations.
  Rising yields (falling futures price) → stronger growth or inflation expectations.
  Falling yields → recession fears or flight to safety.
  Long-term rates drive mortgage costs, investment, and economic activity.
